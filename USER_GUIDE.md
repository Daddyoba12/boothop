# BootHop User Guide

**Version 1.0 · boothop.co.uk**

BootHop connects people who need things delivered internationally with verified travellers already making that journey. No couriers. No warehouses. Just real people helping real people — safely, cheaply, and fast.

---

## Who is BootHop for?

| Role | Who | What they do |
|------|-----|--------------|
| **Hooper (Sender)** | Anyone who needs to send a parcel, document, or gift | Posts a delivery request and pays the traveller |
| **Booter (Traveller)** | Anyone travelling internationally | Carries a parcel on their existing trip and earns money |

---

## Getting Started

### Step 1 — Create your journey

Go to **boothop.co.uk** and choose:
- **"I'm Travelling"** — to earn money by carrying packages on your trip
- **"I Want to Send"** — to find a traveller who will carry your item

Fill in:
- **Route** (e.g. London → Lagos)
- **Date** of travel/delivery
- **Weight** of item (Letter, Small, Medium, or Large)
- **Your price** (what you're willing to pay/accept)

### Step 2 — Verify your email

Enter your email address. You'll receive a **5-character verification code** (e.g. `4827A`). Enter it to log in — no password needed.

### Step 3 — Publish your journey

After logging in, your journey draft will appear on your **Dashboard**. Click **"Publish Journey & Start Matching"** to go live. The matching engine runs automatically.

---

## The Matching Process

BootHop's engine matches senders with travellers based on:

1. **Same route** — e.g. both going London → Lagos
2. **Close dates** — within 3 days of each other
3. **Compatible price** — within 20% of each other
4. **Pickup distance** — origins within 20 miles of each other

When a match is found, **both parties receive an email** with Confirm and Decline buttons. No login needed to respond — the links are secure one-click actions.

### Price Negotiation

If prices differ by more than 20%, BootHop automatically proposes a **fair midpoint**. Both parties receive a negotiation email showing:
- Your offered price
- Their offered price
- The proposed midpoint

Both must click **Accept** for the match to proceed at the midpoint price.

## After a Match

### Confirming the match

Both the sender and traveller must accept. Once both accept:
- Match status becomes **Accepted**
- Both parties are directed to complete **ID verification (KYC)**

### ID Verification (KYC)

Both parties must verify their identity using **Stripe Identity** before contact details are shared. This takes 2–3 minutes:
1. Take a photo of your government-issued ID
2. Take a short selfie
3. Stripe verifies automatically (usually instant)

### Payment

The **sender (Hooper)** pays through BootHop's secure checkout. The money is held in **escrow** — neither party touches it until delivery is confirmed.

**Fees:**
- BootHop charges a small service fee on top of the agreed price
- The traveller receives the agreed amount minus a small platform fee
- Full breakdown shown before you pay

### Contact Details Unlocked

Once all of the following are complete:
- ✅ Both parties accepted the match
- ✅ Payment held in escrow
- ✅ Both KYC verified

…contact details (name and email) are **automatically revealed** so both parties can coordinate pickup and delivery.

## Delivery

### How it works

1. **Sender hands over the item** to the traveller at an agreed pickup point
2. **Traveller carries it** on their journey
3. **Traveller delivers it** to the recipient at the destination

### Confirming delivery

After delivery:
- **Traveller clicks "Confirm I've Delivered"** (via email link or dashboard)
- **Sender clicks "Confirm I've Received It"** (via email link or dashboard)

Once **both confirm**, payment is automatically released to the traveller.

If either party hasn't confirmed, BootHop sends a **reminder email every 6 hours**.

- Personal effects (clothes, shoes, accessories)

- Illegal drugs or controlled substances
- Weapons of any kind
- Counterfeit goods
- Hazardous materials (flammable, explosive)
- Anything that would not clear customs

## Safety & Trust

BootHop is built around a layered safety model:

1. **Email verification** — every account requires a verified email
2. **KYC identity check** — powered by Stripe Identity (government ID + selfie)
3. **Escrow payment** — funds held by Stripe, released only on confirmed delivery
4. **Contact gating** — no personal details shared until ID + payment complete
5. **Dual confirmation** — payment only releases when BOTH parties confirm delivery
6. **Customs compliance** — built-in guidance on what can and cannot be carried

Read the full safety policy at **boothop.co.uk/trust-safety**

## Customs Information

All items must clear customs in both the origin and destination countries. Key rules for UK → Nigeria routes:

- Declare all items truthfully — do not undervalue goods
- Personal effects for own use are generally duty-free
- New goods above the duty-free threshold attract import duty
- NAFDAC restrictions apply to food, medicine, and cosmetics
- FIRS and NCS rules apply at Lagos and Abuja airports

Full customs guide at **boothop.co.uk/how-it-works**

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How is my payment protected?**
Your payment is held by Stripe (one of the world's largest payment processors) in escrow. BootHop cannot access those funds. They are only released once both you and the traveller confirm the delivery is complete.

**What if the traveller doesn't deliver?**
If delivery is not confirmed within the agreed timeframe, BootHop will investigate and initiate a refund process. The dual-confirmation system prevents any party from claiming delivery without the other's agreement.

**What if my item is damaged?**
Both parties confirm condition at delivery. If there is a dispute about item condition, contact support immediately. We recommend taking photos of your item before handover.

**Can I cancel a match?**
Either party can decline a match before both have accepted. Once both have accepted and payment is in escrow, cancellation requests must be raised with support.

**Is BootHop legal?**
Yes. Travellers carrying personal items and gifts on behalf of others is a common and legal practice. All users must comply with customs regulations in both countries. BootHop provides guidance but users are responsible for declaring items correctly.

**What countries do you cover?**
BootHop works on any route where travellers post journeys. The most active corridors are UK → Nigeria (London, Manchester, Birmingham → Lagos, Abuja), but journeys to any destination can be posted.
