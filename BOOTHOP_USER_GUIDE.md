# BootHop — How It Works
### A Plain-English Guide for Users & Business Stakeholders
#### June 2026

---

## WHAT IS BOOTHOP?

BootHop connects two types of people:

- **Senders** — people who need to get a parcel from A to B
- **Travellers** — people already making that journey who have spare luggage space and want to earn money

Instead of using an expensive courier, a Sender pays a Traveller a fraction of the cost to carry their parcel on an existing trip. The Traveller earns extra income from a journey they were already taking. BootHop sits in the middle — making sure the money is safe, the people are verified, and the delivery is tracked.

## THE TWO SIDES OF BOOTHOP

---

### THE SENDER JOURNEY
*"I need to send something"*

---

**STEP 1 — SIGN UP**

The Sender creates a free BootHop account with their email address. They receive a £20 welcome credit on their first booking.

---

**STEP 2 — POST A DELIVERY REQUEST**

The Sender fills in a simple form:
- Where is the parcel going from and to?
- When does it need to arrive?
- What is inside? (general category — e.g. clothing, electronics, documents)
- What is it worth? (declared value — used for insurance)
- How much are they willing to pay?

BootHop automatically checks whether the item is permitted on that route and estimates any customs duties the recipient may need to pay.

---

**STEP 3 — WAIT FOR A MATCH**

BootHop searches for Travellers already heading in the same direction around the same time. When one is found, the Sender gets an email notification.

---

**STEP 4 — REVIEW & AGREE**

The Sender can see the Traveller's route, travel date, and rating. If both sides are happy with the price, the Traveller accepts the match.

> For deliveries going to Africa (Nigeria, Ghana, Kenya, etc.), a BootHop team member reviews the match before the Sender is asked to pay. This usually takes a few hours.

---

**STEP 5 — PAY SECURELY**

The Sender pays via a secure card payment page (powered by Stripe). The money is **held safely by BootHop** — it is not given to the Traveller yet. Think of it like a holding account. The Sender's card is authorised but not charged until delivery is confirmed.

---

**STEP 6 — HAND OVER THE PARCEL**

Once payment is in place, the Sender receives the Traveller's contact details (phone number and email). They arrange the handoff — drop-off point, time, and any special instructions.

Both parties also receive a unique tracking barcode by email.

---

**STEP 7 — TRACK THE DELIVERY**

The Sender can visit a live tracking page at any time to see where their parcel is. They can also "ping" the Traveller for a location update.

---

**STEP 8 — CONFIRM RECEIPT**

Once the parcel arrives, the Sender confirms delivery through their BootHop dashboard with one click. The Traveller does the same.

---

**STEP 9 — DONE**

Once both sides confirm, the payment is released to the Traveller. The Sender is invited to leave a rating and review.

---
---

### THE TRAVELLER JOURNEY
*"I'm travelling and want to earn money"*

---

**STEP 1 — SIGN UP & GET VERIFIED**

The Traveller creates a BootHop account and must complete identity verification:
- Upload a government-issued ID
- Add bank account details for receiving payments
- This is handled securely by Stripe (the same payment company used by Amazon and Deliveroo)

Verification typically takes 24–48 hours. Without it, a Traveller cannot accept deliveries or receive money.

---

**STEP 2 — POST A TRAVEL ROUTE**

The Traveller fills in a simple form:
- Where are they travelling from and to?
- When are they departing?
- How much spare luggage capacity do they have?
- How much do they want to earn?

---

**STEP 3 — GET MATCHED**

BootHop finds Senders who need a parcel delivered on that same route around the same time. The Traveller receives an email with the details.

---

**STEP 4 — ACCEPT OR DECLINE**

The Traveller reviews the parcel details, declared value, and offered price. They can accept, decline, or suggest a different price.

> **Note:** The Traveller can only accept if their identity is verified and their payout account is active. BootHop checks this automatically.

---

**STEP 5 — COLLECT THE PARCEL**

Once the Sender has paid, both parties receive each other's contact details. The Traveller arranges collection of the parcel before their journey.

Both parties receive a QR barcode — the Traveller uses theirs to log updates during the journey.

---

**STEP 6 — CARRY & UPDATE**

As the Traveller moves, they log checkpoints on their BootHop dashboard:
- Parcel collected
- In transit
- Arrived at destination
- Delivered to recipient

If the Traveller goes silent for 48 hours without any update, BootHop's team is automatically notified and both parties are contacted.

---

**STEP 7 — DELIVER & CONFIRM**

The Traveller hands the parcel to the recipient and confirms delivery in their dashboard.

---

**STEP 8 — GET PAID**

Once both the Sender and Traveller confirm delivery, the payment is released to the Traveller's bank account. This typically arrives within 1–2 business days.

## WHAT HAPPENS IN THE MIDDLE

Here is a summary of what BootHop does automatically between the two parties:

| What BootHop Does | Why |
|---|---|
| Verifies the Traveller's identity | So Senders know they are dealing with a real, accountable person |
| Checks the item against customs rules | So nothing illegal or restricted is carried unknowingly |
| Holds the payment in escrow | So the Traveller knows they will be paid, and the Sender knows they won't pay until delivery |
| Tracks activity during the journey | So both parties have peace of mind |
| Alerts the team if a Traveller goes silent | So problems are caught early |
| Only releases payment when both confirm delivery | Protects both parties from fraud |
| Handles disputes if something goes wrong | BootHop mediates and issues refunds if needed |

## PRICING

### What the Sender Pays

| Item | Description |
|---|---|
| Delivery fee | Agreed between Sender and Traveller |
| Platform fee | Small BootHop service charge (3–5%) |
| Insurance | 7.5% of the declared goods value (covers loss/damage) |
| **Total** | **All shown clearly before payment** |

> First-time Senders receive **£20 credit** which is automatically deducted from their first booking.

### What the Traveller Earns

The Traveller receives the agreed delivery fee minus a small BootHop platform commission. Earnings go directly to their bank account after each confirmed delivery.

**Example earnings per trip:**
- Short domestic route: £20–£50
- UK to Europe: £50–£120
- UK to Nigeria / Ghana / Kenya: £85–£320

## WHAT CAN AND CANNOT BE SENT

### Generally Permitted
- Clothing and personal items
- Electronics (phones, laptops, tablets)
- Documents and paperwork
- Food and groceries (non-perishable)
- Gifts and household goods
- Jewellery (subject to value limits)

### Not Permitted — Ever
- Weapons or ammunition of any kind
- Drugs or controlled substances
- Counterfeit or stolen goods
- Live animals
- Explosive or flammable materials
- Cash above certain limits (varies by route)

### Requires Review
- Medicines and pharmaceuticals
- High-value items above £5,000
- Transactions above £10,000 (subject to anti-money laundering checks)

> BootHop uses intelligent screening to check all items before a match is confirmed.

## SAFETY & TRUST

BootHop has **eight layers of protection** built into every delivery:

1. **Identity check** — every Traveller is verified before they can carry anything
2. **Device check** — BootHop detects devices linked to fraudulent accounts
3. **Fraud scoring** — every match is scored for risk before it proceeds
4. **Item screening** — prohibited and high-risk items are flagged automatically
5. **Customs check** — AI checks whether the item is legal on the specific route
6. **Secure payment** — money is held safely, not released until delivery is confirmed
7. **Live tracking** — both parties can see delivery progress in real time
8. **Dual confirmation** — both Sender AND Traveller must confirm before money moves

## IF SOMETHING GOES WRONG

### Traveller Goes Silent
If a Traveller stops responding and provides no updates for 48 hours, BootHop automatically:
- Contacts both parties
- Creates an internal incident report
- Escalates to the BootHop operations team

### Raising a Dispute
Either party can raise a formal dispute through their dashboard within 24 hours of delivery confirmation. BootHop's team reviews the case and decides on one of three outcomes:
- **Full refund** to the Sender
- **Full payment** released to the Traveller
- **Partial resolution** — split between the parties

### Refunds
If a match is cancelled before the parcel is handed over, or a dispute is resolved in the Sender's favour, a full refund is issued to the original payment method. Refunds typically take 5–10 business days to appear.

BootHop also serves businesses that need reliable, on-demand delivery for:
- Time-critical documents
- Spare parts and equipment
- Pharmaceutical or medical items
- Cross-border commercial shipments

Business accounts have access to:
- A dedicated business portal
- Priority delivery tiers with tighter SLAs
- Dedicated account management
- Higher weight and value limits
- Faster dispute resolution

Businesses can apply for **Priority Partner** status for access to the highest service level, including a named account manager and 2-hour support response.
