# BootHop — How It Works
### A Plain English Guide for the Team

---

## What is BootHop?

BootHop is a platform that connects two types of people:

- **Hoopers** — people who need to send goods from one city to another (think: someone in London who needs a package delivered to Manchester)
- **Booters** — people who are already travelling that route and are happy to carry a package for a fee

Instead of using expensive courier services, Hoopers pay a Booter a fraction of the cost to carry their goods on an existing journey. The Booter earns extra money from a trip they were already taking. Everyone wins.

---

## The Journey — Step by Step

---

### STEP 1 — Sign Up & Tell Us Your Journey

Both the Hooper (sender) and the Booter (traveller) visit BootHop and create an account using just their email address. We send them a 6-digit code to confirm it's really them — no passwords needed.

Once logged in, each person posts their journey:

- A **Booter** says: *"I'm travelling from London to Manchester on 15th April, I can carry up to 10kg, and I'd like £30 for the trip."*
- A **Hooper** says: *"I need to send a 5kg package from London to Manchester around 15th April, I'm willing to pay up to £35."*

---

### STEP 2 — Getting Matched

Our system automatically looks for matches every few hours. When it finds a Booter and a Hooper going to the same places around the same time, it sends both of them an email.

The email says: *"We found a potential match! Here are the details. Do you want to proceed?"*

Both parties can **accept** or **decline** with one click directly from their email.

---

### STEP 3 — Agreeing the Price

Once both accept the match, they may need to agree on a final price. Either person can propose a number. When both are happy and click accept, the price is locked in.

---

### STEP 4 — Signing the Agreement

Before anything moves forward, both parties must read and agree to BootHop's Terms & Conditions. This protects everyone. Think of it like signing a simple contract — done with one click on the platform.

---

### STEP 5 — Identity Check (KYC)

For everyone's safety, both the Hooper and the Booter must verify their identity before contact details are shared. This is done through a secure identity check — they take a photo of their ID and a selfie. It takes about 2 minutes.

> **Why do we do this?**
> We need to know who is sending and who is carrying. This protects both parties and keeps BootHop a trusted platform.

Both parties must pass the check before the process continues. If one passes and the other hasn't yet, we wait.

---

### STEP 6 — Payment

Once both identities are confirmed, the Hooper is asked to make payment for the delivery fee.

The Hooper submits their payment details through BootHop. Our team receives a notification and manually confirms receipt of the payment. The money is held safely until delivery is complete — the Booter does not receive anything yet.

> This protects the Hooper — they know their money won't go anywhere until the goods are delivered.
> This protects the Booter — they know payment is secured before they hand anything over.

The Hooper also has the option to declare the value of their goods and take out basic insurance coverage for an additional fee.

---

### STEP 7 — Contact Details Released

Once payment is confirmed by our team, both parties receive each other's **email address**. This is the moment they can actually get in touch to arrange the handover — agree on a time and place to pass the goods.

They can also message each other directly inside BootHop through our secure in-app messaging system (phone numbers and external contact details are not permitted through the chat — this keeps everyone safe and the transaction on-platform).

---

### STEP 8 — The Delivery Happens

The Booter picks up the goods from the Hooper and carries them to the destination city. They arrange the drop-off between themselves.

Our system sends both parties reminder emails asking them to confirm when the delivery is done:

- The **Booter** confirms: *"I have delivered the goods."*
- The **Hooper** confirms: *"I have received the goods."*

Each confirmation is one click from their email. No app needed.

---

### STEP 9 — Payment Released to the Booter

Once both parties have confirmed delivery, our team is notified. We verify everything looks correct and release the payment to the Booter.

Both parties receive a final confirmation email. The transaction is complete.

---

### STEP 10 — Leaving a Rating

After completion, both the Hooper and Booter are invited to rate their experience (1 to 5 stars, with an optional comment). Ratings are used to build trust on the platform — good track records attract more matches.

---

## What Happens If Something Goes Wrong?

### Cancellations

- If a match is cancelled **before payment is made**, it's free with no penalties.
- If a match is cancelled **after payment is submitted**, the Hooper receives a 90% refund within 3–5 business days. A small fee is retained to cover processing.

### Disputes

If there is a problem — goods not delivered, goods damaged, miscommunication — either party can raise a **dispute** directly from the platform. Our team reviews the case and makes a fair decision. Possible outcomes include:

- Full payment to the Booter (delivery was completed)
- Full refund to the Hooper (delivery failed)
- Split payment (partial fault on both sides)
- No action required (misunderstanding resolved)

Both parties are notified of the decision by email.

---

## Our Role as BootHop

We are the **trusted middleman**. We:

- Match the right people together
- Verify everyone's identity
- Hold the money safely until delivery is confirmed
- Mediate if anything goes wrong
- Keep all communication on-platform for everyone's protection

We do not share anyone's personal details until payment is secured and both identities are verified.

---

## Revenue Model

BootHop earns money by:

1. **Taking a platform fee** from each successful transaction (built into the agreed price)
2. **Insurance fees** — optional cover on the declared value of goods (taken at payment stage)

## Summary in One Paragraph

A Hooper posts what they need to send. A Booter posts their travel route. BootHop matches them. Both agree a price and sign terms. Both verify their identity. The Hooper pays — BootHop holds the money. Only then do both parties get each other's contact details. They coordinate the handover. Both confirm delivery is done. BootHop releases the money to the Booter. Both leave a rating. Simple, safe, and fair for everyone.
